Darwin Day 2025

By Matt Young
January 22, 2025 12:00 MST

Darwin Day, variously known, I think, as Evolution Day and later Evolution Weekend and Religion and Science Weekend, commemorates Darwin’s birthday on February 12, 1809 (yes, the same day as Abraham Lincoln).

The National Center for Science Education warns us that Darwin Day approaches and announces:

For its part, NCSE, with the National Association of Biology Teachers and Katie Hinde, will be reprising the popular evolution symposium "A Lioness Walks Into an Orca: How Stories Enhance Science Education" and sharing exciting information about the 2025 March Mammal Madness tournament online at 7:00 p.m. Eastern on February 12, 2025 (details to be announced). And of course, NCSE will be active on social media in support of evolution education — look for (and use) the hashtag #whyteachevolution on Darwin Day, and check out our #whyteachevolution essays.

I had to search a little harder to find other events. Here they come:

Evolution 2025 is the joint meeting of the American Society of Naturalists, the Society of Systematic Biologists, and the Society for the Study of Evolution. The meeting is one of the premiere international opportunities for sharing research on evolutionary biology. “The conference will be held in 2 parts: A 2 day virtual conference with live online workshops, talks, symposia, and networking events May 29-30, followed a few weeks later by a 5 day in-person conference in Athens, GA, USA at the Classic Center, June 20-24, 2025.”

International Darwin Day listed four events:

February 12, 7:00-8:30 pm, Darwin Day Lecture: "Twists and Turns in Teaching Evolution over the Years, Calkins Science Center - Grand Rapids Community College , 226 Bostwick Ave. NE, Grand Rapids, MI, United States.

February 12, 7:00-9:00 pm, Darwin Day, "Evolution und Medizin," Siegmund Freud Universität, Freudplatz 3, Wien, Austria.

February 13, 6:00-7:30 pm, "The Past is the Key to the Future: Oregon’s Fossil Rodents and the Impacts of Environmental Change," The Museum of Natural and Cultural History, 1680 E. 15th Ave., Eugene, OR, United States.

April 4-5, Iowa City Darwin Day: Science Fest, three speakers for talks on Friday, April 4 and Saturday, April 5.

You may find URL's and further information on the Darwin Day website.

Date and time not given. The Gene’s Eye View of Evolution, J. Arvid Ågren, evolutionary biologist and author of The Gene’s-Eye View of Evolution, J.F. Crow Institute for the Study of Evolution. Also art contest.
