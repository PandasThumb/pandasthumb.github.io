According to <a href="https://hummingbirds.vetmed.ucdavis.edu/information/facts">this source</a>, a hummingbird might flap its wings with a frequency of 50-90&nbsp;Hz, though less when it is hovering. Mr. Dayton, who apparently knows his stuff, set his camera for F/2.8 and ISO 50, and achieved a shutter speed of approximately 1/3300&nbsp;s, or 3300&nbsp;Hz, which ought to be fast enough to stop the motion of the wings (according to the metadata associated with the photograph I received). Below, I will post another of his pictures, which shows the coloration of the bird somewhat better. I will confess that I cropped both pictures and adjusted the exposure ever so slightly.
